NGS and Its analysis

Next Generation Sequencing (NGS) is a transformative technology that has revolutionized scientific research. Unlike traditional methods that required years to decode a single genome, NGS can sequence entire genomes within days. This groundbreaking technology employs massively parallel sequencing, simultaneously analyzing millions of small DNA fragments, allowing researchers to investigate thousands of genomic regions at single-base resolution in a single experiment, empowering them to tackle ambitious questions and achieve comprehensive insights.

NGS is compatible with a wide range of sample types, making it a versatile tool for exploring diverse biological questions. It supports the unbiased investigation of multiple biological “omes,” including the genome, transcriptome, proteome, and epigenome, providing a holistic view of biological systems. Moreover, NGS facilitates multiomics approaches, examining multiple omes simultaneously to offer a comprehensive understanding of complex biological interactions. This powerful technology is essential for advancing our knowledge and addressing critical challenges in biology and medicine.
